Simple Present Tense

Used for habits, routines, and general facts.

  • Formula: Subject + Verb 1 (+ s/es for he/she/it)
  • Example: She walks to school every day.

Present Continuous Tense

Used for actions happening right now.

  • Formula: Subject + is/am/are + Verb-ing
  • Example: They are watching a movie.

Simple Past Tense

Used for actions completed in the past.

  • Formula: Subject + Verb 2
  • Example: He visited Bali last year.

Future Tense

Used to describe future events.

  • Formula: Subject + will + Verb 1
  • Example: I will call you tomorrow.

Present Perfect Tense

Describes actions that happened at an unspecified time and are still relevant.

  • Formula: Subject + has/have + Verb 3
  • Example: We have finished our homework.

Modal Verbs

Auxiliary verbs that express ability, permission, necessity, etc.

  • Common modals: can, could, should, must, may, might
  • Example: You should study more often.
